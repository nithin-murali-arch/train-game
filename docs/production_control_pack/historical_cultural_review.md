# Historical and Cultural Review Guide

## Purpose

The setting is colonial India (1850s–1947). The cargo list includes historically loaded goods: opium, indigo, tea, cotton, colonial extraction. This gives the game personality and strategic depth, but also risk. This document defines boundaries for responsible representation.

## Tone Boundaries

### What the game IS
- A logistics and economics strategy game
- Satirical about corporate incentives and monopolies
- Historically flavored, not historically accurate
- Aware of colonial power imbalances without being didactic

### What the game is NOT
- An endorsement of colonialism
- A trivialization of famine, exploitation, or forced labor
- A caricature of any culture or people
- A game that uses real-world tragedies as disposable jokes

## Handling Colonial Exploitation

The game includes colonial companies and extraction mechanics. The systems should **imply** unequal power through mechanics, not through explicit narrative:

- Port bonuses reflect extraction economies
- Toll regimes reflect control over trade
- Administrative contracts favor established powers
- Unrest events reflect real tensions

Do NOT:
- Glorify the British Raj as benevolent
- Present colonialism as purely efficient or modernizing
- Use slurs or dehumanizing language for any group
- Make light of specific historical atrocities

## Cargo and Commodities

### Opium
- Historically: British East India Company opium trade to China caused immense harm
- In game: included as high-value cargo for strategic trade-offs
- Rule: Present as "trade good" in UI. Do not glorify. Do not show consumption. Do not make jokes about addiction.

### Indigo
- Historically: Indigo plantations in Bengal involved exploitative labor
- In game: medium-value dye crop
- Rule: Neutral description. No plantation imagery that romanticizes labor.

### Tea
- Historically: British tea industry displaced indigenous agriculture
- In game: high-value frontier cargo
- Rule: Present as commodity. Avoid "civilizing" narratives.

### Cotton / Textiles
- Historically: Deindustrialization of Indian textile production under British rule
- In game: core manufactured good
- Rule: Acknowledge Dacca's historic textile role without editorializing.

## Events

### Monsoon / Flood
- Real and deadly in Bengal
- In game: logistical challenge
- Rule: Frame as weather event affecting infrastructure. Do not show human casualties.

### Labor Strike / Unrest
- Reflects real labor movements and resistance
- In game: operational pressure
- Rule: Treat strikers as rational actors, not villains. Settlement option is valid and sometimes optimal.

### Famine
- Bengal famines (1770, 1943) were catastrophic
- In game: If included, frame as "famine relief contract" — logistics of moving grain to affected areas
- Rule: NEVER show famine as entertainment. NEVER use sensational images. Focus on logistics, not suffering.

## Place Names

Use names appropriate to the colonial era while acknowledging modern equivalents:

| Era Name | Modern Name | Usage |
|----------|------------|-------|
| Calcutta | Kolkata | Acceptable alternate; use Kolkata in UI |
| Dacca | Dhaka | Acceptable alternate; use Dacca in UI |
| Patna | Patna | Same |
| Murshidabad | Murshidabad | Same |
| Bombay | Mumbai | Use Mumbai if mentioned |
| Madras | Chennai | Use Chennai if mentioned |

## Satirical Corporations

Satirical corporate houses (Amdani, Amboney, Tota, Mahendra) are allowed:
- The joke is about **incentives and monopolies**, not communities or individuals
- Do not mock real people's names, families, or religious identities
- Keep satire **systemic**: corporate behavior, not personal attacks

## Language to Avoid

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "Savages" | Never use |
| "Civilizing mission" | Never use |
| "White man's burden" | Never use |
| "Natives" | "Population," "Workers," "Citizens" |
| "Coolie labor" | "Workers," "Laborers" |
| "Famine entertainment" | "Relief logistics," "Supply challenge" |
| "Chaos everywhere!" (sensational) | Clear, factual event descriptions |

## Review Checklist

Before shipping any text, image, or event:

- [ ] Does this trivialize historical suffering?
- [ ] Does this glorify colonial power?
- [ ] Does this mock a real community or identity?
- [ ] Would this be offensive to someone from Bengal or India?
- [ ] Is the tone strategic/satirical rather than cruel?
- [ ] Are laborers and populations treated with dignity?

If any answer is yes, revise before shipping.

## When in Doubt

Choose the more respectful option. The game can be sharp about corporate behavior without being sharp about people.

The tone target:

> "The British East India Rail is efficient and ruthless. That is the joke. The people who built the actual railways were not the joke."
